\begin{question}
    
\textbf{Exercise 1 - Leapfrog}

Study the leapfrog scheme for the advection equation.

a) Give the leapfrog scheme (centered scheme with 2nd order accuracy in space and time).

b) Derive the stability criterion.

c) Discuss the computational mode and how it can be avoided.

\textbf{Exercise 2 - Upwind}

Study the upwind scheme for the advection equation.

a) Give the upwind scheme (uncentered scheme with 1st order accuracy in space and time).

b) Derive the stability condition.

\textit{HINT: The upwind scheme consists of a forward and backward part!}

\textbf{Exercise 3 - Euler forward}

Study the Euler forward scheme for the diffusion equation.

a) Derive the stability criterion of the Euler forward scheme for the diffusion equation:

\[
\frac{u^{n+1}_j - u^n_j}{\Delta t} = A \frac{u^n_{j+1} - 2u^n_j + u^n_{j-1}}{(\Delta x)^2}.
\]

b) Discuss how the time step should be chosen. Why is it good to choose a smaller time step than that given by the stability criterion? Hint: Study how the amplification factor depends on the wavelength, especially the highest frequencies.

c) Are the oscillations that may appear in this scheme a numerical mode?

\end{question}
